Site History

(Taken from the about page.)

My interest in HTML or technology in general has been very present thru out my life, I used to spend hours working on my Planet Minecraft, DeviantArt, and forum profiles. When user customization started to phase out of popularity (Or becoming pay-walled for us 12 year olds) I moved to more standard SNS platforms. This was a very odd period of my life where I realize I was watering down my personality and things I liked to try and find community. I became inspired to create a website after finding Gundham being shared in a chat I was in. Something about the site really struck a chord with me, it felt like its own little realm and handcrafted world. Something I craved, individuality. If that Webmaster is out there somewhere, I just want to say thank you.

Since that day on August 4th 2021, my website has gone thru many revisions and changes. This is not my first website, nor the first time I've used Neocities as a hoster actually. When I was younger, around 11 years old, I dabbled with a shitty webpage with some of my friends. It was nothing revolutionary, just Gorillaz and Ace Attorney fanart. I'd put the center tag at the start of *every* line to ensure it was centered... shivers. I didn't continue since I didn't feel very inspired to, or saw a point. Sometime in 2020, I created a website named "theinfirmary", which was again mostly for piddling around with learning HTML. I don't recall what made me want to make this site? Nonetheless, the only surviving evidence I have of it is this screenshot from Discord when I was testing out the description.

I deleted it after finding out it gained 500 views, which freaked me out that people saw my dinky test website. (Which was nothing to worry about looking back on it now) The only notable thing on it was a photo of Thrax from Osmosis Jones, when you hovered over him I called him hot or something. I also created another website called "aceattorneyvillains" on August 19th 2021, a fansite for the villains from the Ace Attorney series; which I worked on more for a brief while before this site. I worked on it while I was rather sick to pass time, and as you can infer by the front page if you go seeking it, its archived. The links are all broken now from me not being very wise and using hotlinking (Which is what you deserve by the way) and I'm just too lazy to delete it right now. So view it thru the waybackmachine if you want to.

Eventually I shifted focus back onto my personal website, which you're at(!), but I also maintain other sites you can find on the front page. Such as the Crashbox Database I started on October 14th 2023. Previously, this URL was "blaisedebeste", which is the name of my favorite character in the Ace Attorney series. And then shifted to "gyakutenkenji2", which is the game the previous character is from; and my favorite in the series. And finally, it has been "boothworldindustries", and will likely stay that way.

I don't have many remarkable things to say about the site's early days. Most of which I was unable to find screenshots or proper archival of; likely due to its rapidly changing nature. Considering I started this site when I was a teen, I had alot of social anxiety with how I presented myself. And I had the opinions of people who really didn't care about my interests. Which is to say, cringy, it was alot more "carrd"-like. As seen by the earliest iteration here.

The layout here is from sometime in 2022, and was when I first really started getting involved with the "indie web" community. I ended up keeping this layout for quite a bit, and it was one of my first major progressions when I first started learning HTML. And I learned my favorite element you still see me use today, "aside". :]

A really funny story about this time period, was that I used to get harassment from some people in the Ace Attorney fanbase. (In case you're curious why, it was just because I liked the major antagonists) I had some people in my cbox acting like fools (Whats with the cbox always bringing in the most bold people?) and I sent them back their IPs. Suffice to say, I have not been bothered since.

Concepts

In mid-late 2023 I became incredibly unsatisfied with the current index of the website. I did enjoy the "desktop" feeling I had created, but the gimmick of a OS styled website was getting a bit limiting. And I simply found the current appearance to be flimsy and uninspired overtime.

After a night of browsing the web design museum I decided to take a crack at drawing a more ambitious layout. The idea was I'd draw a "frame"/"skeleton" and use z-index to make it appear like screens. This was the first attempt I did at it, and it fit so perfectly I went with it.


Here was the previous attempt at an index. The idea was to have three different "portals" at the index. I never liked this idea, but I did try to get it to work for a while.

Misc Management and Fun Facts

Boothworld is maintained locally on my laptop, and backed up onto USB occasionally. You can view the source on Github here. Since June 2024, the site is coded in VSCode.

Most gifs are found using Gifcities, or from the original Shaye Saint John website. Graphics are edited in FireAlpaca or using ditherit. Many textures are taken from textures.neocities.org.

Webrings I created were made using onionring.
