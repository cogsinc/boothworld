She also has additional lore in Tome 6, "Divergence".

Athough the slowest walking Killer in the game, Nurse makes up with her "blink" ability, titled "Spencer's Last Breath". Nurse is like a ghost, and able to teleport thru walls and floors, at the cost of being fatigued for a second afterwards. She can surprise Survivors by quickly zipping to them.

Nurse has three special perks native to her, being: "A Nurse's Calling", which allows her to see those healing nearby. "Stridor", which increases the noises of breathing and pain Survivors make. And "Thanatophobia", which slows Survivor progress for every one injured or hooked.

She was first introduced in the free DLC titled "The Last Breath", released August 18th 2016.

My loadout for Nurse consists of "Unrelenting", "Sloppy Butcher", "Scourge Hook: Monstrous Shrine", and either "A Nurse's Calling" or "Lightborn". "Unrelenting" is great for her slower speed and recovering from missed hits. "Sloppy Butcher" and "A Nurse's Calling" work great together for finding Survivors that think they got away.

I also like using the "Torn Bookmark" and "Ataxic Respiration" or "Fragile Freeze" for add-ons.

Nurse was the first Killer I decided to play as, I'm unfortunately predictable with Nurse-oriented characters. I was unaware of her apparent skill ceiling, it did take me a while to get used to how far her blinks can go. (..And avoiding teleporting to another floor by accident.) My biggest problem was getting less stingy with her blinks. Although funnily I found that people expecting blinks tends to mess with their predictability.

Some of my favourite cosmetics for her would have to be the red "On Probation", "WW2 Uniform", "Miss Smithson" and her mask from Twisted Masquerade.

Although Nurse is one of my most played Killers, I'm not the biggest fan of her outfits. I prefer to keep her face masked; but I do like her red hair. I also funnily don't like when her arms are exposed, it feels weirdly bare especially in first person.

Reaching the warden's office, Caleb kicked the door and was met with a fortunate sight — it wasn't only the prison warden who cowered in a corner, but Henry Bayshore. Soaked in blood and sweat, Caleb hobbled to his old cell, hardly paying notice to Bayshore's screams. He sat on the bed's edge as drops of blood ran from his fingertips. He also has additional lore in Tome 18, "Revision".

Armed with his self-made ability titled "The Redeemer", Deathslinger is a range based killer. Aiming down his sights, he can spear and reel in Survivors who don't respect his line of sight. Targets who break free of his chain are inflicted with a deep wound, taking extra time to heal.

Deathslinger has three special perks native to him, being: "Dead Man's Switch", which locks up and marks a generator Survivors stop repairing after hooking someone. "Gearhead", after a Survivor loses a health state he can see the aura of anyone performing a good skill check for the next 30 seconds. And "Hex: Retribution", which inflicts oblivious on Survivors cleansing or blessing a totem, and then reveals their auras for 15 seconds.

He was first introduced in the DLC titled "Chains of Hate", released March 10th 2020.

My loadout for Deathslinger consists of "Hex: Retribution", "Hex: No One Escapes Death", "Scrouge Hook: Monstorous Shrine", and "Dead Man's Switch". The two hexes work together to punish anyone during end game, and "Dead Man's Switch" is great for information. Occasionally I do swap it for "A Nurse's Calling" however, great for after causing deep wound.

I also use "Iridescent Coin", "Barbed Wire", "Warden's Keys", or "Wanted Poster" for add-ons. I think they're all really good.

Deathslinger was the first character I decided to unlock after grinding shards! I was immediately drawn to him, and hes probably my favourite character in the game. It took me a while to get used to the slight delay of his shots, but hes just so much fun to play as. I also love getting Slingers in my lobby when I'm Survivor. <3

Some of my favourite cosmetics for him would be "Frontier Vigilante", "King of Hearts", and "Double-Crossed". But I really just like his default recolors, and keeping his longer pale hair.

Wielding his "Wailing Bell", the Wraith can vanish and manifest at its ringing call. Quickly traversing the map while cloaked, he leaves Survivors anxious for his whereabouts.

Wraith has three special perks native to him, being: "Bloodhound", which brightens pools of blood left by Survivors. "Predator", increasing the closeness of scratch marks left behind. And "Shadowborn", giving haste when blinded.

He was introduced alongside the launch of the game. Alongside Trapper and Hillbilly as the original Killer trio, on June 14th 2016.

My loadout for Wraith consists of "Scrouge Hook: Monstorous Shrine", "A Nurse's Calling", "Barbecue & Chili", and "Sloppy Butcher". Information perks really shine for his "hit and run" play-style. His speed while cloaked is a great tool for swapping focus and keeping pressure.

For add-ons, you really can't go wrong with any. But I mainly use "Shadow Dance ~ Blood", "Swift Hunt ~ Blood". Both of his iridescent add-ons are amazing, but I tend to run out quickly.

Wraith was the other Killer I started out playing as, when I wanted something less intensive as Nurse. And still I find him a consistent choice thats incredibly fun; I have a bit of a sentimental attachment to him now- People can really underestimate him, his body blocking can catch people off guard, and hes quick to move or destroy loops. I really want to try "Bamboozle" on him.

Pattern you've probably noticed, is that I really just like most default outfits. But I have to say I think his "The Angel of Music" is one of the best in game. And I'm not just saying that because I'm a Wraith/Nurse truther.
